Hi! My name is Joshua, and like many beginners, I spent years searching for the "perfect" way to learn programming. I tried Khan Academy, Codecademy, Codédex, freeCodeCamp, Boot.dev, and many other resources. Every platform had something valuable to offer. Yet I kept running into the same problems: Many courses spent too much time on toy examples before helping you build something meaningful. The number of tutorials, bootcamps, and learning paths made it difficult to know where to start. Even after completing lessons, I often understood the syntax better than I understood how to actually use the technology to solve problems. In short, these platforms were built by professionals who were trying to teach beginners, but they were missing the perspective of someone who was actually learning. I wanted to learn by building real projects, but I didn't know how to get started. I wanted to understand how technology works, but I didn't know where to look. I wanted to solve real problems, but I didn't know what problems were worth solving. FirstCommit exists to make that journey to building real meaningful projects easier.
